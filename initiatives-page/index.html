TRYAMBAKAM, Alwar

2019

With a mission to promote the preservation of natural resources and indigenous practices, NSS DTU organized its annual Rural Camp from 20th to 25th December 2019, at Gopalpura, Alwar. Among other activities like learning about organic farming practices, setting up library facilities at the community school, offering 'Shramdaan,’ and many more, the volunteers interacted and learned from the 'Waterman of India', Sh. Rajendra Singh regarding various water preservation and river revival techniques.

villages of Bheekampura and Gopalpura, Rajasthan

2018

Our Rural Camp'18 was in the villages of Bheekampura and Gopalpura,Thanagazi tehsil, Alwar district in the state of Rajasthan from December 15,2018 to December 20, 2018. This camp was attended by 36 volunteers of NSS- DTU who were passionate about understanding the problems faced by people at the grassroot level of our country, the places where the benefits of modern education, technology and the rapid economic growth have not yet percolated. Yet, they were also aware of the immense value these villages had in teaching us about the sustainable manner of living in tune with the ways of nature- something the developed world seems to be in dire need of. Though the goal of this camp was to broaden our understanding of the conditions prevailing in these villages by living with them for some time, we were also successful in making some contributions to the village by means of solar panel installation and informative artwork in the community hall. Bheekampura is the village which serves as the headquarters for Tarun Bharat Sangh, an environmental NGO currently working in the field of water harvesting using innovative indigenous methods. They have been successful in mitigating the problem of water scarcity to a large extent in the drought prone state of Rajasthan. Gopalpura is an adjacent village housing a community of farmers and their families.

Village Harchandpur Kalan

2017

Our Rural Camp'17 was in the village Harchandpur Kalan, Sakit Block (Etah district, UP) from December 27th, 2017 to January 1st, 2018. This camp was attended by 34 volunteers of NSS-DTU with a mission to bring about ground-level changes in the village and to understand the problems of rural India from the roots. Every NSS volunteer had summoned in their minds to stand up to their motto “Not Me But You”, by striving for selfless service to the village with full dedication and determination. We focussed on certain issues of prime importance such as Open defecation, Hygiene, Education, Career, govt. scheme implementation, etc. by organising various activities in the village. Harchandpur Kalan is a small village in the Etah district of western Uttar Pradesh with a population of approximately 1500 people. The most common occupations are farming, labour and cattle rearing.The camp was organised in collaboration with an NGO, Jaago India which is a people’s movement for building leaders among the society. Jaago India has been working for the development of Harchandpur since the past few months.

village Gadhaiya, UP

2016

Gadhaiya is a small village in the Rampur district of western Uttar Pradesh with a population of approximately 2000 people. The most common occupations are farming and labour. The camp was organised in collaboration with an NGO, Jaago India which is a people’s movement for building leaders among the society. Jaago India has been consistently working for the development of Gadhaiya. The volunteers of National Service Scheme, Delhi Technological University (NSSDTU) made serious efforts to serve the purpose of their motto “Not me, but you” at the Special Rural Camp. Forty seven volunteers reached the village on the night of October 7 and were welcomed warmly by the villagers. The activities started early on the morning of the 8th of October. The camp activities aimed to increase our students’ knowledge and skills in social work practice by interacting with the rural community, learning about rural lifestyle, team spirit, sharing, planning and organizing programs etc as it provided ample opportunities for the students to fine tune their interpersonal relationship skills through the process of group dynamics. The students spread awareness, information, and organized various programs for the community during the rural camp.
